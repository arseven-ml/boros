How it's calculated

Position chain (capital → position):
margin = equity × Prime Broker leverage  ·  Prime Broker loan = margin − equity  ·  total notional = margin × exchange leverage  ·  per-leg = notional / 2

Income: gross = spread × notional × days/365; slippage = 2 × (bps/10000) × (per-leg/$1M) × notional × days/365

Costs: Prime Broker interest = rate × loan × days/365; exchange fees = 4 × taker × per-leg; Boros swap = 2 × tier × per-leg × days/365; Boros OI = 2 × OIfee × per-leg × days/365; Boros margin = 2 × IM × per-leg × days/365, opp. cost = margin × Prime Broker rate × days/365

Return: net = income − all costs; capital = equity + Boros margin; ROE = net × 365/days / capital; waterfall ROI % = component$ / initial equity (period figure).

Note: the slippage formula is reproduced exactly from the v3 sheet (it applies a 2-leg factor to the full 2-leg notional). If that is a double-count, true slippage cost is about half — ask and this can be switched.
