How it's calculated

Position chain: margin = equity × Prime Broker leverage · Prime Broker loan = margin − equity · total notional = margin × exchange leverage · per-leg = notional / 2

Income & slippage: gross = spread × notional × days/365. Slippage is not a guessed input — each leg's per-leg fill is run against that market's real Boros order book (24h-average depth, 25 May 2026). The average rate paid climbs as the fill eats through the 30 bps and 120 bps depth bands; slipCost = leg1 + leg2.

Costs: Prime Broker interest = rate × loan × days/365; exchange fees = 4 × taker × per-leg; Boros swap = 2 × tier × per-leg × days/365; Boros OI = 2 × OIfee × per-leg × days/365; Boros margin = 2 × IM × per-leg × days/365, opp. cost = margin × rate × days/365

Return: net = income − all costs; capital = equity + Boros margin; ROE = net × 365/days / capital.

Order-book depth is a 25 May 2026 snapshot — real liquidity moves intraday. The book model is piecewise-linear through the 30 bps / 120 bps depth bands.
